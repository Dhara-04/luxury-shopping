How to Measure

For accurate measurements, follow these guidelines:

  1. Bust: Measure around the fullest part of your bust, keeping the tape measure horizontal.
  2. Waist: Measure around the natural waistline, keeping the tape slightly loose.
  3. Hips: Measure around the fullest part of your hips, approximately 8 inches below your waist.

When taking measurements, stand naturally with feet together. Wear form-fitting clothing rather than bulky layers.

How to Measure

For accurate measurements, follow these guidelines:

  1. Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape measure horizontal.
  2. Waist: Measure around your natural waistline, keeping the tape slightly loose.
  3. Inseam: Measure from the crotch to the bottom of your ankle.

When taking measurements, stand naturally with feet slightly apart. Wear form-fitting clothing rather than bulky layers.

Shoe Sizing Tips

For the perfect fit, consider these recommendations:

  1. Measure your feet at the end of the day when they are at their largest.
  2. Always measure both feet and fit shoes to the larger foot.
  3. There should be about a half-inch of space between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.
  4. Consider the type of socks you'll be wearing with the shoes.

Accessories Sizing Tips

For the perfect fit, consider these recommendations:

  1. Belts: Measure around your waist where you typically wear your belt.
  2. Hats: Measure around your head about 1/8" above your ears.
  3. Gloves: Measure around your dominant hand at the widest point excluding thumb.
  4. Rings: Measure your finger at the end of the day when it's likely to be largest.

Pro Tip

When between sizes, we recommend sizing up for a more comfortable fit, especially for tailored items.

Fabric Consideration

Natural fibers like cotton and silk may have less stretch than synthetic blends. Keep this in mind when selecting your size.
